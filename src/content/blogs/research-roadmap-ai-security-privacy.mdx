## Focus

My current research direction sits at the intersection of AI security, privacy,
and trustworthy machine learning. The central question is simple to state and
hard to solve: how do we build AI systems that remain useful when the data,
model, or surrounding environment may be adversarial?

## Themes

- Security and privacy risks in modern AI systems
- Adversarial machine learning and model robustness
- Applied cryptography for privacy-preserving computation
- Decentralized system security
- Practical evaluation methods for trustworthy AI

## Why It Matters

Machine learning systems increasingly make decisions in sensitive settings.
Accuracy alone is not enough when models can leak information, fail under
carefully designed inputs, or behave unpredictably outside their training
distribution.

The long-term goal is to connect theoretical guarantees with systems that can
be measured, deployed, and trusted in practice.
